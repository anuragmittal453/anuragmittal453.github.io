My current research focuses on proprioceptive active perception — enabling robots to infer object geometry and contact states using intrinsic sensing, uncertainty-aware exploration, and interaction-driven reasoning. I believe the future of robust manipulation lies in what robots feel, not just what they see.

Active Research
Proprioceptive Active Perception for Reflective/Transparent Objects
▼
Intrinsic joint torque sensing combined with uncertainty-driven next-best-view planning for manipulation of transparent and reflective objects — without external tactile hardware or dedicated F/T sensors. The robot uses joint torque residuals (τ_ext = τ_meas − τ_model) as the sole proprioceptive channel, closed-loop with a greedy entropy-minimising NBV planner on a wrist RGB-D camera.

Vision-Free Active Tactile Exploration
▼
Vision-free active tactile exploration framework for planar surface edge detection and reconstruction using only intrinsic end-effector force-torque sensing of the Franka FR3 — no dedicated tactile skin. GP-based active probing with UCB/MI acquisition, three-phase impedance probe controller, 6-phase grasp loop with slip detection, and place verification.

▼
